# Paywall & Upgrade Screen CRO

Optimize in-app paywalls, upgrade screens, upsell modals, or feature gates. Distinct from public pricing pages — this focuses on in-product upgrade moments where the user has already experienced value.

## Initial Assessment

Before providing recommendations, understand:

1. **Upgrade Context**
   - Freemium → Paid?
   - Trial → Paid?
   - Tier upgrade?
   - Feature upsell?
   - Usage limit?

2. **Product Model**
   - What's free?
   - What's behind paywall?
   - What triggers prompts?
   - Current conversion rate?

3. **User Journey**
   - When does this appear?
   - What have they experienced?
   - What are they trying to do?

---

## Core Principles

### 1. Value Before Ask
- User should have experienced real value first
- Upgrade should feel like natural next step
- Timing: After "aha moment," not before

### 2. Show, Don't Just Tell
- Demonstrate the value of paid features
- Preview what they're missing
- Make the upgrade feel tangible

### 3. Friction-Free Path
- Easy to upgrade when ready
- Don't make them hunt for pricing
- Streamlined checkout flow

### 4. Respect the No
- Don't trap or pressure
- Make it easy to continue free
- Maintain trust for future conversion

## Paywall Trigger Points

### Feature Gates

When user clicks a paid-only feature:
- Clear explanation of why it's paid
- Show what the feature does
- Quick path to unlock
- Option to continue without

**Feature gate copy formula:**
```
[Feature name] is available on [Plan]

[Visual preview or description of feature]

[What it helps you do / benefit]

[Upgrade CTA]  [Maybe Later]
```

### Usage Limits

When user hits a limit:
- Clear indication of limit reached
- Show what upgrading provides
- Don't block abruptly—give options

**Usage limit copy formula:**
```
You've reached your [limit type] limit

[Visual: progress bar at 100%]

Free: [current limit] | Pro: [upgraded limit or "Unlimited"]

[Upgrade CTA]  [Alternative action: delete/archive]
```

### Trial Expiration

When trial is ending:
- Early warnings (7, 3, 1 day)
- Clear "what happens" on expiration
- Summarize value received during trial

**Trial expiration sequence:**

**7 days before:**
- Gentle reminder
- Highlight features they've used
- "Still time to explore"

**3 days before:**
- More urgent
- What they'll lose
- What they've accomplished

**1 day before:**
- Final reminder
- Clear CTA
- Support offer

**Expired:**
- Clear status
- Easy upgrade path
- Downgrade option

### Time-Based Prompts

After X days of free use:
- Gentle upgrade reminder
- Highlight unused paid features
- Easy to dismiss
- Don't show to recently dismissed users

### Milestone-Based Prompts

After achieving something:
- Celebrate their success
- Show how paid could amplify
- Natural upgrade moment

## Paywall Screen Components

### Essential Elements

1. **Headline** - Focus on what they get
   - "Unlock [Feature] to [Benefit]"
   - "Get more [value] with [Plan]"
   - NOT: "Upgrade to Pro"

2. **Value Demonstration**
   - Preview of feature
   - Before/after comparison
   - "With Pro you could..."
   - Sample output or result

3. **Feature Comparison**
   - Highlight key differences
   - Current plan marked
   - Focus on value, not features
   - 3-5 items max (not exhaustive)

4. **Pricing**
   - Clear, simple pricing
   - Annual vs. monthly options
   - Savings highlighted
   - Per-seat clarity if applicable

5. **Social Proof**
   - Customer quotes
   - "X teams use this"
   - Relevant to upgrade decision

6. **CTA**
   - Specific and value-oriented
   - "Start Getting [Benefit]"
   - "Unlock [Feature]"
   - NOT: "Upgrade Now"

7. **Escape Hatch**
   - Clear "Not now" or "Continue with Free"
   - No guilt-trip copy
   - Easy to find and click

## Timing and Frequency

### When to Show

**Good timing:**
- After value moment (aha!)
- After achieving milestone
- When hitting genuine limits
- When trying paid feature
- After positive experience

**Bad timing:**
- During onboarding (too early)
- When they're in a flow
- Right after showing one
- During critical tasks
- When frustrated

### When NOT to Show
- First session (usually)
- During complex workflows
- After recent dismissal
- To users who've declined multiple times
- When user is in support/help mode

### Frequency Rules
- Limit impressions per session (1-2 max)
- Cool-down after dismiss (days, not hours)
- Track annoyance signals (rage clicks, immediate dismiss)
- Reduce frequency for repeated declines
- Reset after significant product changes

### Frequency Caps

| Context | Recommended Cap |
|---------|-----------------|
| Feature gate (same feature) | Once per day |
| Feature gate (different) | 2-3 per session |
| Soft upgrade prompt | Once per week |
| Trial expiration | Follow sequence |
| Usage limit | When hit + daily until resolved |

## Upgrade Flow Optimization

### From Paywall to Payment

**Minimize friction:**
- Keep in-context if possible
- Pre-fill known information
- One-click upgrade for simple plans
- Clear pricing (no surprises)

**Payment flow:**
- Simple plan selection
- Clear billing terms
- Trust badges visible
- Easy to go back

### Post-Upgrade Experience

**Immediate:**
- Instant access to features
- Confirmation and receipt
- Celebration moment

**Guided:**
- Tour of new features
- Suggested next actions
- Quick win with new capability

**Follow-up:**
- Welcome email
- Getting started resources
- Check-in after 7 days

## Measurement

### Key Metrics

| Metric | What it tells you |
|--------|-------------------|
| Paywall impression rate | How often users see upgrade prompts |
| Click-through rate | Interest in learning more |
| Upgrade conversion rate | Impressions → Purchases |
| Time to conversion | How long from first impression |
| Revenue per impression | Efficiency of prompts |

### Segment Analysis

Compare conversion by:
- Trigger type (feature gate vs. limit vs. prompt)
- User tenure (new vs. established)
- Usage level (light vs. heavy)
- Feature attempted
- Device/platform

### Funnel Analysis

```
Paywall Impression → Click → Checkout Start → Payment → Upgrade Complete
      100%             30%        20%            15%          12%
```

Identify biggest drops.

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

### Dark Patterns (Never Do)
- Hiding the close button
- Confusing plan selection
- Guilt-trip copy ("Don't you want to succeed?")
- Fake urgency/scarcity
- Pre-checked upsells
- Difficult cancellation

### Conversion Killers
- Asking before value delivered
- Too frequent prompts
- Blocking critical user flows
- Complicated upgrade process
- Unclear pricing
- No way to continue free

### Warning Signs
- High dismiss rate (>90%)
- Support complaints about prompts
- User churn after seeing paywall
- Negative reviews mentioning upselling

## A/B Testing Ideas

### What to Test

**Trigger timing:**
- Earlier vs. later in user journey
- After specific actions vs. time-based
- Different thresholds for limits

**Headline/copy:**
- Benefit-focused vs. feature-focused
- Specific vs. general claims
- Urgency vs. no urgency

**Price presentation:**
- Monthly vs. annual default
- Total vs. per-user pricing
- With/without comparison to free

**Design/layout:**
- Modal vs. inline vs. full-page
- Minimal vs. detailed
- With/without feature comparison

**Social proof:**
- With testimonials vs. without
- Stats vs. quotes
- Industry-specific vs. general

## Experiment Ideas

### Trigger Experiments

**Timing**
- Show at limit hit vs. approaching limit
- After X actions vs. after X days
- After specific feature use vs. general usage
- First session vs. later sessions

**Context**
- In-flow paywall vs. dedicated upgrade page
- Blocking vs. dismissible
- Full-screen vs. modal vs. banner

---

### Messaging Experiments

**Headlines**
- Benefit headline vs. feature headline
- Personalized vs. generic
- Question vs. statement

**Value Proposition**
- Feature list vs. benefit list
- Before/after comparison
- ROI/savings calculator
- Customer success story

**Copy Tone**
- Direct vs. soft sell
- Urgency vs. no pressure
- Personal vs. professional

---

### Design Experiments

**Layout**
- Minimal (headline + CTA) vs. detailed
- Vertical vs. horizontal plan comparison
- Feature comparison visible vs. expandable

**Visuals**
- With product preview vs. without
- Screenshots vs. illustrations
- Video demo vs. static

**CTA**
- Button text variations
- Single CTA vs. multiple options
- Color and prominence

---

### Pricing Display Experiments

**Presentation**
- Monthly vs. annual default
- Per-seat vs. total
- With savings callout vs. without
- Single plan vs. plan comparison

**Anchoring**
- Show higher tier first vs. lower
- Include enterprise option vs. hide
- Compare to cost of problem
